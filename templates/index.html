Background

Lagos State is introducing the Certified Accreditor Programme as part of the reforms to restore confidence in the building construction industry by ensuring that all building construction projects within the state are adequately monitored by Certified Accreditors - professionals with proven capabilities and requisite experience. Building construction accreditation, as encapsulated in the Certified Accreditor Programme, will be carried out by these private-sector professionals duly registered with the respective bodies. These Certified Accreditors would assess the relevant designs/drawings contained in each application and thereafter approve the drawings, undertake site inspections, issue authorisation for work to proceed, and certification for work stages satisfactorily completed. What is CAP?

Certified Accreditor Program (CAP) is an initiative designed to enable the Lagos State Government engage private sector professional as stakeholders in regulating the built environment. Q.

How can I become a Certified Accreditor (CA)?

A Certified Accreditor (CA) is a group of professionals that come together to form a Consortium. All CAs must have a minimum of 4 Registered Professionals depending on the grade

Q.

Can an already existing / registered Company register as a CA?

Yes, the Firm only need to provide proof having the required professionals as members of the Firm

Q.

Who are the professionals qualified for CAP?

i. A Registered Architect ii. A Registered Civil/Structural Engineer iii. A Registered Mechanical Engineer iv. A Registered Electrical Engineer v. A Registered builder vi. A Registered Town Planner vii. A Registered Geoscientist
