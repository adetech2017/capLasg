CERTIFIED ACCREDITORS PROGRAM

The Program is designed to enable the Lagos State Government engage private sector professionals as stakeholders in regulating work processes in the built environment. These partners will be known as CERTIFIED ACCREDITORS (CAs) These Certified Accreditors (CAs) will work with the Ministry and its Agencies, LASPPPA and LASBCA to do the following:

  1. Screening of Technical Drawings
  2. Stage Certification
  3. Random Monitoring

A Certified Accreditor would be a Consulting Firm, or a group of registered professionals made up of some or all of the following professionals.

  1. A Registered Architect
  2. A Registered Civil/Structural Engineer
  3. A Registered Mechanical Engineer
  4. A Registered Electrical Engineer
  5. A Registered builder
  6. A Registered Town Planner
  7. A Registered Geoscientist
  8. Other Relevant Professional in the Built Environment

These Professionals shall have cognate post-registration experience and demonstrable skills to undertake these project monitoring works and shall have the responsibility of document review and construction inspection staging on behalf of the Ministry of Physical Planning and Urban Development.

To register for the Certified Accreditor Programme, a team of professionals must commit to working together and thereafter apply as a Team. An Expression of Interest letter jointly signed by all members of the team would be required, amongst other details/documents.

GRADING SYSTEM

Building construction projects have been categorized into groups on the basis of project complexity and thus determines the grade of project supervision/monitoring required. 4 grades have been defined as described below:

Grade 1: Specialized Projects: This category will define projects that are primarily non-building structures and those that require specialized skills for both design and construction. These shall include structures such as Steel structures, auditorium roof structure, electrical and electronic mast structures, Petrochemical facilities and gas stations, highway steel sign/walkway structures and billboard support structures etc.

Grade 2: 11 Floors and above: These are building structures classified as mid-rise to high-rise structures. The accreditors shall review the different building systems in each development and ensure that the building development meets the Nigerian Building Code standards and the departments’ building development regulations. These standards including the structural frame system, the water & wastewater system, the electrical system and the other building services systems.

Grade 3: 5 Floors to 10 Floors: These building structures are those that are usually classified as low to mid- rise structures. The accreditors shall review the different building systems in each development and ensure that the building development meets the Nigerian Building Code standards and the departments’ building development regulations.

Grade 4: 1 Floor to 4 Floors: These building structures are those that are usually classified as low rise structures. The accreditors shall review the different building systems in each development and ensure that the building development meets the Nigerian Building Code standards and the departments’ building development regulations. This category represents the large portion of current building development and shall be regulated by the LASBCA personnel alongside the Accreditor Professionals Each group of Accreditors’ team shall submit a signed agreement letter of cooperation during application with information on each member professional competency as well as each member membership status of their respective regulatory body.
